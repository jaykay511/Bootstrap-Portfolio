About Me

I was ambitious, though I didn't know exactly what I was shooting for. Now I think it's one of the most useless questions an adult can ask a child - What do you want to be when you grow up? As if growing up is finite. As if at some point you become something and that's the end.
Becoming - Michelle Obama

"What do you want to be when you grow up?"

Adults often ask this vexing and complex question to kids. Throughout elementary and middle school my answers varied from "Librarian" to "Professional Athlete" to "Store Clerk" - the latter because scanning barcodes seemed like a lot of fun.

In high school, with the help of the guidance counselor, I realized I had an aptitude and appreciation for hospitality. I learned about and worked in restaurants, hotels, tour operators, etc. while pursuing my undergraduate degree at New York University.

"So, what do you do?"

When you grow up... your major... what do you do...The wording changes, but people still ask these questions because, well, they're trying to understand who you are. These days I'm a student again, learning full-stack development in order to find a position where I can utilize my experience. The goal is to find work where all my favorite things intersect: teamwork, hospitality, integrity, and joie de vivre.

Coding has become something I get lost in. As a detail-oriented problem-solver, getting code to work properly feels like a victory every time. I look forward to continuing to learn and adapt, just as web development continues to evolve, and using that knowledge to build wonderful things!
